## Magical Birthday Origins 
The origins of birthday celebrations are not a recent concept and are not exclusive to Christianity. Scholars trace the tradition of celebrating birthdays back to ancient pagan times. Historical records suggest that the ancient Romans were among the first to celebrate the birthdays of common people. In Roman tradition, men were honored with a cake on their fiftieth birthday, and the custom of placing birthday candles on cakes is believed to have originated from ancient Greece. These candle-lit cakes were offerings to goddess Artemis, and blowing out the candles was a way to send prayers to the goddess. As you blow out your own candles this year, consider sending your wish to the patron god, goddess, or saint associated with your birthday. 

When we consider the spiritual meaning of birthdays, we recognize that the day of one's birth inherently represents a liminal time and space. This signifies being on the threshold of another year or cycle around the sun. Liminal space holds profound power as it opens us up to the spiritual realm. Alongside this, liminal space, and consequently birthdays, were historically regarded as a "dangerous" period, when spirits and energies could easily influence one's life. The tradition of gathering as a group to celebrate an individual's birthday served as a form of protection. Interestingly, singing, clapping, and creating loud noises were believed to ward off evil spirits and bad luck during these celebrations.

## 6. Lastly, Consider Your Birth Month and Day of the Week 
The month in which you are born holds its own mystical significance. Take, for instance, the birth month of May. How does this month bear magical connotations? May derives its name from the Roman goddess Maia, known for her portrayal as a nurturing and affectionate mother in Roman mythology. Maia was revered as the mother of Hermes and was known to extend her care to other deities who had been forsaken by their mothers. The name Maia is believed to signify "larger" or "greater," hence linking the goddess Maia to the proliferation of plants, creatures, trees, and the womb. As a result, those born in May are believed to be under the protective and loving influence of the earth goddess.
