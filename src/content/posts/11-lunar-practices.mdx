So, you might be wondering--is a witch just a witch? The answer is yes… and also no.

While witches often share common threads in their practices and beliefs, many find themselves naturally drawn to a specific style of magic that reflects who they are. Some feel most at home working with plants, seasons, and the natural world as green witches. Others weave magic into everyday life through food, intention, and hearth-centered rituals as kitchen witches. Beyond those, there are hedge witches, cosmic witches, gray witches, crystal witches, and many more--each path shaped by different focuses, energies, and ways of relating to magic.

Lunar witches center their magic around the moon--its cycles, phases, and quiet influence over emotions, intuition, and energy. They move with the waxing and waning of lunar light, using the moon as both a guide and a mirror. In the sections ahead, we’ll explore what defines lunar witchcraft, the beliefs that often accompany it, and how lunar witches intentionally work with each phase of the moon in their magical lives.

So settle in, look to the sky, and let’s step into the soft glow of lunar magic together.

## What Makes a Lunar Witch
To begin, it helps to clarify what we mean by the word witch. At its core, a witch is someone who consciously works with magic to create change--within themselves, their environment, or their lived experience. With that in mind, a lunar witch is a practitioner who places special emphasis on the moon, its energy, and its ever-shifting phases.

That doesn’t mean a lunar witch works only with the moon. Many draw from a wide range of magical tools, traditions, and energy sources. The defining difference lies in where their attention returns again and again. For the lunar witch, the moon is a primary guide, a steady rhythm, and a trusted source of insight.

Let’s take this a step deeper.

Lunar witchcraft often involves intentionally aligning spells, rituals, and reflections with the phases of the moon--such as the waxing and waning crescents, the full moon, and the new moon. Each phase carries its own symbolism and energetic quality, and lunar witches learn to work with those natural shifts rather than against them. In doing so, intentions are supported by the same cycles that govern tides, growth, release, and renewal.

For many lunar witches, this connection extends beyond simply tracking moon phases. Some also feel drawn to lunar deities and mythological figures associated with the moon, finding inspiration, protection, or guidance through these archetypal energies. Honoring these lunar beings can add a deeply spiritual and reverent layer to their practice, connecting modern magic with ancient wisdom.

Lunar witches are often naturally inclined toward practices like moon gazing, astrology, and forms of divination that highlight lunar influence. These practices help deepen awareness of how the moon subtly shapes emotional states, intuitive insights, and energetic flow. Through this awareness, lunar witches learn how to channel lunar energy in ways that feel empowering, restorative, and aligned.

By honoring the moon’s cycles and weaving its energy into their magic, lunar witches cultivate a profound relationship with both the natural world and the cosmos. In following the moon’s constant dance of change, they embrace a timeless rhythm--one that reminds them that transformation is not only natural, but necessary.

## Signs You're a Lunar Witch
If you’ve found your way here, there’s a good chance you already sense a quiet pull toward lunar magic--or at least a curiosity that keeps resurfacing. Some people discover their magical path through study, while others simply recognize themselves in it. Lunar witchcraft often feels less like a choice and more like a remembering.

Here are a few signs that may point to a natural connection with the lunar path:

You’ve always felt a deep attraction to the Moon--not just as a celestial body, but as something meaningful, comforting, or even sacred. You may find yourself watching it rise, tracking its phases, or feeling a sense of awe when it’s especially bright.

You notice emotional, energetic, or even physical shifts that seem to align with the Moon’s cycles. Full moons may feel intense or activating, while new moons bring introspection, rest, or a desire to reset.

You feel drawn to lunar deities or have worked with them before. This might include figures such as Hecate, Selene, Artemis, Diana, Thoth, Manu, or other moon-associated beings across cultures and traditions.

When the full moon appears, you can’t help but point it out. You feel compelled to share its beauty, pause to admire it, or step outside just to stand beneath its glow.

You may belong to the zodiac sign Cancer, which is ruled by the Moon, or have strong Cancer placements in your birth chart. Even without Cancer energy, heavy lunar influence elsewhere in your chart can create the same pull.

You find yourself preferring silver over gold--whether in jewelry, tools, or symbolism. Silver has long been associated with the Moon, intuition, and reflective energy, and many lunar witches naturally gravitate toward it.

If several of these resonate with you, it doesn’t mean you must identify as a lunar witch--but it may explain why moon-based practices feel intuitive, grounding, or emotionally rich. Lunar magic tends to call softly, but persistently, inviting you to listen rather than rush.

And if you’re listening now, you’re likely already closer to the lunar path than you think.

# 11 Lunar Magick Practice Suggestions 
Now that we’ve laid the groundwork, it’s time to move into the heart of this post: lunar practices you might feel called to explore. Before we begin, it’s important to say this clearly--there is no right or wrong way to practice lunar magic. This section isn’t here to correct, gatekeep, or redefine your relationship with the Moon.

What I’m sharing here is simply how I personally work with the lunar phases--what has felt supportive, intuitive, and meaningful over time. You may find that some of these practices resonate deeply with you, while others don’t at all. That’s completely okay. Think of these suggestions as invitations rather than instructions.

With curiosity, openness, and a gentle mindset, you might discover a few ideas you’d like to weave into your existing practice--or perhaps feel inspired to begin a lunar practice of your own. Take what feels right, leave what doesn’t, and trust your intuition as your guide.

Let’s begin.

### Synchronizing Your Practice with the Lunar Phases
One of the simplest ways to work with lunar magic is by aligning your practice with the Moon’s natural cycles. Rather than forcing energy or timing, you allow the rhythm of the Moon to guide when you reflect, build, release, and rest.

For example, you might begin shadow work or deep self-reflection around the time of the new moon. This phase is ideal for turning inward, gaining clarity about your true desires, and setting intentions that feel honest and aligned. As the Moon moves into the waxing phase and toward the first quarter, those intentions can begin to take shape through gentle action and focus.

When the full moon arrives, many lunar witches shift into practices centered on manifestation, gratitude, and energetic amplification. This is a powerful time to acknowledge growth, celebrate progress, and fully step into what you’ve been cultivating. As the Moon begins to wane, attention naturally turns toward release--letting go of emotional weight, stagnant patterns, or anything that no longer serves your well-being.

By moving through these phases intentionally, your practice becomes less about effort and more about flow--mirroring the same cycles of expansion and release that the Moon models for us each month.

### Engaging with Lunar Deities
Those who walk a lunar path often feel a natural pull toward moon deities--sometimes as a quiet curiosity, other times as a deep, persistent calling. These deities embody different expressions of lunar energy, from intuition and protection to mystery, transformation, and divine timing.

Moon goddesses frequently honored in lunar magic include Artemis, Hecate, Selene, Diana, Devana, Luna, and the Wiccan Triple Goddess, who represents the Maiden, Mother, and Crone aspects of the Moon. Lunar gods may include Thoth, Mani, and Khonsu, each carrying their own symbolism, stories, and energetic qualities.

Working with lunar deities does not require formal worship, rigid devotion, or lifelong commitment. Connection can be as subtle or as structured as feels right to you. Some lunar witches honor deities through offerings, prayer, or altar work, while others simply study their myths, meditate on their symbolism, or invite their presence during specific moon phases.

The most important part of this practice is consent--both yours and theirs. Building a relationship with a lunar deity should feel supportive, curious, and respectful rather than pressured or performative. You may find that one deity resonates strongly at a particular phase of life, while another fades into the background. That ebb and flow mirrors the Moon itself.

Engaging with lunar deities can add depth, mythic context, and spiritual companionship to your lunar practice--but it is always optional. Trust your intuition, move slowly, and allow any connection to unfold naturally over time.

### Cultivating a Moon Garden and Herbalism 
If you have a green thumb--or even just a curiosity about plants--creating a moon garden can be a beautiful way to deepen your lunar practice. A moon garden doesn’t need to be large, elaborate, or demanding. It can be as simple as a few potted plants on a windowsill, balcony, or porch. What matters isn’t the size, but the intention behind it.

Many plants and herbs are associated with lunar energy or tend to bloom and thrive under moonlight. Moonflowers, for example, open their blossoms at night, while herbs like mugwort have long been linked to the Moon and are often used in lunar magic, dream work, and intuition-focused practices. Growing or working with these plants can help you feel more attuned to the Moon’s quieter, subtler rhythms.

If you decide to start a moon garden, it’s a good idea to research which plants grow well in your specific climate or growing zone. You may even discover native plants that naturally bloom at night or carry lunar symbolism, making them especially aligned with your practice. Working with plants that are already suited to your environment helps keep this practice low-effort and sustainable.

Once established, your moon garden can become a living part of your magic. You might harvest herbs during specific moon phases, sit nearby during moon gazing rituals, or simply tend to the plants as a form of meditative, grounding care. Even observing their growth over time can strengthen your connection to lunar cycles.

It’s important to remember that not all plants are safe to handle or consume. Some herbs associated with lunar magic can be toxic if used incorrectly. Always research thoroughly before harvesting, ingesting, or preparing any plant, and when in doubt, keep your work symbolic rather than physical.

A moon garden is less about perfection and more about relationship--between you, the plants, and the Moon overhead. Let it be gentle, intuitive, and shaped by what feels manageable and meaningful to you.

### Lunar Nourishment and Culinary Magic
Can lunar magic be woven into the food and drinks you enjoy every day? Absolutely. Every ingredient we consume comes from the earth, and nearly all plants carry associations with celestial bodies--including the Moon. By becoming more intentional with what you prepare and consume, nourishment itself can become a quiet but powerful lunar practice.

Start simply by paying attention to the vegetables, fruits, herbs, and teas you already enjoy. Many of these are traditionally linked to lunar energy, intuition, emotional balance, and cycles of growth. Incorporating them more mindfully--whether through cooking, brewing, or even choosing them with intention--can turn everyday meals into subtle acts of magic.

You might also choose to prepare something special during a meaningful Full Moon. This doesn’t have to be extravagant. It could be a thoughtfully cooked meal, a shared dish, or even a simple dessert made with presence and care. Foods that visually echo the Moon--round loaves, cakes, pancakes, cookies, cheese, fruits like melons or citrus--can feel especially symbolic and fun to work with during this phase.

As you cook, consider setting a gentle intention, expressing gratitude for the ingredients, or simply acknowledging the Moon overhead. Eating with awareness--rather than rushing--helps anchor lunar energy into the body, making nourishment both physical and energetic.

Lunar culinary magic isn’t about strict recipes or rules. It’s about slowing down, honoring cycles, and remembering that care--especially the care we offer ourselves--is one of the most accessible forms of magic there is.

### Magic of the Water Element 
Because of its deep relationship with the Moon, the lunar witch often feels a natural resonance with the element of water. The Moon governs tides, emotional flow, intuition, and subconscious movement--making water one of the most accessible and supportive elements for lunar magic.

If you’ve never intentionally worked with elemental magic before, water is a wonderful place to begin. It doesn’t demand complexity or physical effort, and it adapts easily to your energy level. Water magic can be as simple or as layered as you want it to be, meeting you exactly where you are.

A water-based ritual might be as gentle as brewing a cup of tea with intention, infusing it with calm, clarity, or emotional release. Taking a bath or shower with awareness--perhaps during a specific moon phase--can also become a form of lunar water magic, allowing stress, heaviness, or stagnant energy to wash away.

Even small acts count. Drinking water mindfully, creating moon water, washing your hands with intention, or sitting near a natural body of water can all strengthen your connection to this element. These practices remind us that magic doesn’t always look dramatic--it often moves quietly, like water itself.

By working with water alongside the Moon, lunar witches learn to honor emotional cycles, practice release without force, and trust the natural ebb and flow of their inner world.

Do you know your moon sign--or have you ever explored what it represents? While most people are familiar with their sun sign (often simply called "your sign"), everyone also has a moon sign, and it plays a deeply personal role in astrology.

Your moon sign reflects your emotional landscape, inner needs, intuitive responses, and the way you process feelings beneath the surface. It speaks to how you experience the world internally, rather than how you present yourself outwardly. For lunar witches especially, understanding the moon sign can feel like uncovering a missing piece of the puzzle.

Once you know your moon sign, you may start to notice patterns--emotional rhythms, sensitivities, or intuitive strengths--that align closely with lunar cycles.

Working with your moon sign can help you tailor your lunar practice in a way that feels more supportive and authentic. Whether that means choosing rituals that honor your emotional needs, timing practices to certain moon phases, or simply understanding yourself more compassionately, this knowledge can deepen your connection to both the Moon and your inner world.

Knowing your moon sign isn’t about labeling yourself--it’s about listening more closely to what already lives within you.

### Birthday Moon Phase 
Your moon phase is different from your moon sign, and the two are often confused. While your moon sign reflects where the Moon was positioned astrologically at the time of your birth, your moon phase refers to the actual phase the Moon was in on the day you were born. These are two separate, but equally meaningful, pieces of lunar insight.

If you’re unsure what your birth moon phase is, you can easily find it by entering your birth date into a lunar phase calculator, such as the one available at MoonGiant.

Understanding the moon phase you were born under can offer surprisingly personal insight. Many people find they feel especially aligned with their birth phase--energized, grounded, reflective, or expansive in ways that mirror its qualities. In my own experience, I’ve always felt particularly invigorated and powerful during the New Moon. It wasn’t until I learned that I was born under a New Moon that this connection fully clicked into place.

Now, I intentionally incorporate New Moon energy into my magical practice, using it as a time for renewal, intention-setting, and inner alignment. Knowing your birth moon phase can help you do the same--highlighting which lunar energies naturally support you and which phases may feel most potent or comforting.

Working with your birth moon phase isn’t about limiting yourself to one moment in the cycle. Instead, it’s about recognizing a familiar rhythm within the larger lunar dance--and allowing that rhythm to guide and strengthen your magic.

### Lunar Moon Water  
Have you ever made moon water? If not, this is a wonderful practice to explore--and one of the most accessible ways to work directly with lunar energy. Moon water is created by allowing water to sit beneath the Moon, absorbing the energy of a specific phase, season, or zodiac influence.

Once you’re familiar with the basics, don’t be afraid to experiment. Try making moon water during different moon phases and notice how each one feels. A New Moon batch may feel quiet and introspective, while Full Moon water can feel charged and expansive. Seasonal shifts and zodiac signs can add even more nuance, giving each batch its own subtle personality.

You can also infuse your moon water with intention by adding crystals, herbs, or a pinch of salt--always ensuring that anything you place in the water is safe and appropriate for its intended use. Over time, you may start to notice differences in how each variation feels or how you’re drawn to use it.

Moon water is incredibly versatile. You might use it to cleanse your altar or magical tools, add it to ritual baths, mist your space, water plants, or even include it in household routines like laundry or floor washes. Each use becomes a quiet way of carrying lunar energy into your everyday environment.

Like most lunar practices, moon water doesn’t require perfection. It’s about observation, curiosity, and relationship. Let yourself play, take notes if you’re inclined, and trust your intuition as you learn what each batch wants to be used for. Over time, moon water can become a familiar and deeply personal part of your lunar practice.

### Ritual Baths 
Ritual baths are a beautiful blend of cleansing, relaxation, and quiet empowerment, and they’re a favorite practice for many lunar witches--including myself. They offer a way to slow down, reconnect with your body, and tend to your energy in a gentle, restorative way.

If your bathroom has a window, consider opening it and allowing moonlight to filter in while you bathe. Even a glimpse of the Moon or the night sky can deepen the experience and help you feel more attuned to lunar energy. This simple act can turn an ordinary bath into a sacred pause.

Ritual baths are especially effective for soothing frazzled nerves, clearing lingering emotional residue, and resetting your energetic frequency. They’re often used for aura cleansing, emotional release, and grounding after intense periods or difficult days. Many practitioners also incorporate ritual baths into dedication or initiation rites--similar in symbolism to a baptism--as well as workings related to attraction, self-love, abundance, and personal renewal.

Because water is so closely associated with the Moon, ritual baths naturally act as a bridge between your inner world and lunar energy. Whether your bath is carefully prepared with herbs, salts, and oils, or kept intentionally simple, the power lies in presence and intention rather than complexity.

A ritual bath doesn’t need to be elaborate to be meaningful. Even a quiet soak with mindful breathing and a clear intention can be deeply transformative, reminding you that rest itself is a powerful form of magic.


### Divination by Moonlight 
Practicing divination beneath the Moon can feel deeply otherworldly, as though the veil between intuition and insight grows thinner in lunar light. There’s something about moonlight that naturally quiets the mind and heightens awareness, making it an ideal setting for receiving messages and guidance.

You don’t need much to begin--just your preferred divination tool and a soft light source, such as a candle or lantern, to support visibility. Tarot cards, oracle decks, pendulums, and runes can all feel especially responsive when read under the Moon’s glow, as lunar energy tends to amplify intuition and emotional clarity.

Some lunar witches find that mirror scrying or water scrying becomes particularly potent during the Full Moon. The reflective nature of these practices mirrors the Moon itself, creating a symbolic and energetic resonance that can deepen visions, impressions, or intuitive flashes. Even sitting quietly and gazing into still water or a darkened mirror can invite subtle insights to surface.

Divination by moonlight doesn’t have to be elaborate or perfectly timed. What matters most is presence--allowing yourself to slow down, observe, and listen. Whether your reading feels vivid and clear or soft and symbolic, trust that lunar divination often speaks in whispers rather than declarations.

Under the Moon, intuition leads. All you need to do is follow.

### Your North Node and South Node 
Beyond your moon sign, there are other parts of your birth chart that carry strong lunar significance--two of the most meaningful being your North Node and South Node. These points aren’t planets, but they hold powerful insight into your inner world, emotional patterns, and long-term growth.

Your North Node reflects the direction your soul is learning to move toward in this lifetime. It speaks to growth, purpose, and the qualities you’re meant to develop over time. This includes your evolving passions, natural talents waiting to be claimed, and lessons that stretch you beyond your comfort zone. Many people associate the North Node with soul growth that unfolds gradually through lived experience.

Your South Node, in contrast, represents familiarity. It points to ingrained habits, instincts, and patterns that feel emotionally safe or automatic. These traits often come easily to you, but relying on them too heavily can sometimes keep you anchored in the past rather than moving forward. The South Node can also reflect emotional imprints carried across time--whether interpreted psychologically, spiritually, or symbolically.

Exploring your North and South Nodes can bring greater awareness to the emotional rhythms you carry and how they interact with lunar energy. By understanding where you instinctively retreat and where you’re being gently encouraged to grow, you deepen your relationship with the Moon as a guide--not just of cycles in the sky, but cycles within yourself.

Working with these aspects of your chart allows lunar magic to become more intentional and self-aware, helping you honor both where you’ve been and where you’re becoming.
